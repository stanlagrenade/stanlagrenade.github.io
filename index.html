Introduction

Allow me to introduce myself, My name is Stanley Lagrenade and I am an aspiring Software Engineer

My name is Stanley Lagrenade. I grew up in New York but I don’t have a New York accent until you ask him to play the “Tell me you’re from NY, without telling me youre from NY” game and ask me to say the word “water”. I have been living in the DC area since 2002 when I arrived here for school at Georgetown University. I ran track in college and later coached at my alma mater for three years. I left the coaching world in 2011 and began my career in the tech industry where I managed software projects in the music industry, education sector, and federal government. I have worked in the facial recognition industry for the last few years as a Solution Engineer where I oversaw the development and deployment of facial recognition systems in airports as well as in other applications. I have spoken at numerous conferences on facial recognition technology and its applications in the world around us. During my time at NEC, I was responsible for making Atlanta airport the first fully biometric terminal in the world. As a result, fliers only need their face to access the airport from curb to gate.

Outside of work, I still continue to run and even do track workouts to stay in shape since I am a player/coach for the Georgetown alumni flag football team. After coaching and covering young whippersnappers all weekend, I usually turn my attention to the three Bs, brunching, baking, and bourbon. I am the self-proclaimed Bourbon Baker and am usually in the kitchen finding ways to create new dishes using bourbon. Sadly, my wife doesn’t like Bakery or Bourbon, so I’m left to do my own QA after baking all weekend. Who says developers should never QA their own products? :-)
